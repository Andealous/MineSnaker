The goal of minesweeper is to find all the mines in the minefield.

You start the game with a 16x16 grid of squares, this is the minefield.
There are 40 mines randomly distributed in the minefield.

If you click one of the squares you will start at a mine-free zone.
And you will see that the squares on the edges of that zone will have numbers on them.
These numbers tell you how many mines are ajacent to it.
So if it says 3, that means there are 3 mines around it.

If you think a cell is free you can clear it by clicking it.
This will reveal a number on the cell you cleared.
This number again is how many mines are around that cell.

If you are sure a cell contains a mine you can place a flag on it.
Placing a flag is done by clicking the "flag" button on the bottom of the screen and then clicking the cell you want to mark.

But if you clear a cell with a mine instead of marking it, the mine will explode.
So try to think before clicking a cell.

When you marked all the mines with a flag*, YOU WIN!

* only the bombs, if the total flags are above 40 you still have to deselect the ones that aren't bombs
